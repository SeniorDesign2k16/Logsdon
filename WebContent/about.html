The Gene Spot

Bioinformatics and Evolutionary Biology Tool


About The Gene-Spot

The Gene-Spot is an application designed to cluster related genes among organisms across evolutionary time and provide biological researchers with relevant information for local analysis/annotation.

Currently, annotating the vast amounts of genetic information contained in public & private databases can takes months to complete and must be accomplished manually. Additionally, there is no current standard for displaying this kind of information (further complicating annotation) and are no reliable methods of data verification, making it difficult to transfer results from lab to lab. The Gene-Spot automates parts of this process, vastly increasing the speed at which data can be acquired and analyzed. Pertinent information is then organized and stored in a new database, ready to update and/or distribute.


Team members include: Anubhav Balakrishnan, Rae Corrigan, Brendan Dudley, and Austin Ward [University of Iowa - BME-Bioinformatics]
